Earth's Major Systems

Explore the complex systems that make our planet unique and habitable.

Hydrosphere

All of Earth's water, including oceans, lakes, rivers, groundwater, and glaciers.

Atmosphere

The layers of gases surrounding Earth that protect life and regulate climate.

Lithosphere

Earth's rigid outer layer, including the crust and upper mantle.

Biosphere

The global ecological system integrating all living organisms and their interactions.

Earth's Structure

Crust

5-70 km thick, continental (granite) and oceanic (basalt) types.

Mantle

2,900 km thick, semi-solid rock that flows slowly over time.

Outer Core

2,200 km thick, liquid iron and nickel generating Earth's magnetic field.

Inner Core

1,250 km radius, solid iron-nickel alloy with temperatures over 5,000°C.

Our Solar System

The Sun and everything bound to it by gravity, including planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.

The Sun

Our G-type main-sequence star, containing 99.86% of the solar system's mass.

Inner Planets

Terrestrial planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars - rocky with solid surfaces.

Outer Planets

Gas giants (Jupiter, Saturn) and ice giants (Uranus, Neptune).

Dwarf Planets

Including Pluto, Ceres, Haumea, Makemake, and Eris.

Voyager Program

Launched in 1977, now in interstellar space, carrying the Golden Record.

Mars Rovers

Hubble Telescope

Launched in 1990, revolutionized astronomy with stunning deep-space images.

James Webb

Infrared telescope launched in 2021, studying the early universe.

Seven Natural Wonders

Recognized for their extraordinary natural beauty and significance.

Great Barrier Reef

World's largest coral reef system off Australia's coast, visible from space.

Grand Canyon

Massive gorge in Arizona carved by the Colorado River over millions of years.

Aurora Borealis

Natural light display in polar regions caused by solar particles.

Parícutin Volcano

Mexican volcano that suddenly appeared in a cornfield in 1943.

Victoria Falls

Largest waterfall in Africa on the Zambia-Zimbabwe border.

Harbor of Rio

Stunning natural harbor surrounded by dramatic granite mountains.

Mount Everest

Earth's highest peak at 8,848 meters in the Himalayas.

Biodiversity Hotspots

Amazon Rainforest

Largest tropical rainforest with unparalleled biodiversity.

Madagascar

Island with unique wildlife found nowhere else on Earth.

Coral Triangle

Marine area in Southeast Asia with highest coral diversity.

Cape Floristic

South African region with exceptional plant diversity.
